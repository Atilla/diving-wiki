# Papua New Guinea (Kimbe Bay, Milne Bay)

## Status

- Hit by 2024 event (Alert Level 3) but from an exceptional baseline. Low human pressure = faster recovery.
- Kimbe Bay: 413 species of hard coral (over half all known world species). Geographic isolation and low tourist pressure are protective.
- **One of the best-value, least-crowded, most biodiverse liveaboard destinations.** Narrow seasonal windows.

## Key Dive Sites

### Kimbe Bay (New Britain)

Reefs grow on submerged volcanic seamounts, producing steep-walled pinnacles and bommies rising from deep water — topography that concentrates nutrients, current, and marine life into compact sites.

#### Susan's Reef

Seamount topping at ~3m, walls dropping to 40m+. Enormous barrel sponges, gorgonian fans, dense soft coral. Resident barracuda school, grey reef sharks on deeper walls, pygmy seahorses on Muricella fans.

#### Inglis Shoal

Offshore seamount, top ~5m, current-swept. The premier big-fish site — grey reef sharks, silvertip sharks, schooling jacks, dogtooth tuna. Visibility often 30m+. Intermediate to advanced.

#### Bradford Shoals

Deep offshore pinnacle (top ~12m). Hammerhead sharks encountered seasonally. Pelagic action with barracuda and rainbow runners. Advanced due to depth and current.

#### Restorf Island

Sheltered fringing reef, 5-25m. Outstanding macro: nudibranchs, Coleman shrimp on fire urchins, mantis shrimp. Good night diving.

#### South Emma Reef

Massive reef with swim-throughs and overhangs, 5-30m. Schooling fusiliers, anemone cities, resident turtle population.

#### Joy's Reef

Shallow bommie (3-18m) with some of Kimbe's densest soft coral growth. Excellent wide-angle photography.

Spinner dolphin pods (often 50-200 animals) are regularly encountered on surface intervals.

### Milne Bay

The southeastern tip of mainland Papua New Guinea. The draw is diversity of diving styles — walls, muck, and coral gardens within one trip.

#### Observation Point

Current-swept point with grey reef sharks, barracuda, eagle rays along a steep wall. 10-35m.

#### Dinah's Beach

World-class muck diving on black volcanic sand. Mimic octopus, wonderpus, Ambon scorpionfish, rhinopias, flamboyant cuttlefish, hairy frogfish. 3-15m, calm conditions. A strong alternative to [Lembeh Strait](indonesia/lembeh.md).

#### Samarai Area

WWII wrecks and reef sites around the former colonial island. Soft coral gardens, mandarin fish at dusk.

#### Deacon's Reef

Pristine hard coral walls, turtles and reef sharks.

### Other Liveaboard Areas

#### Eastern Fields

Remote atoll system ~150km offshore in the Coral Sea. Pristine walls, large shark populations (silvertips, grey reef, occasional hammerheads), virtually zero other divers. One of the most remote dive destinations on Earth.

#### Witu Islands

Between Kimbe Bay and the Bismarck Sea. Healthy reefs with little traffic, good for wide-angle reef scenics and pelagics.

#### Fathers Reefs

Seamounts north of Kimbe with strong currents and big-fish action. Often included on Kimbe itineraries.

## Marine Life

- **Sharks**: Grey reef, whitetip, silvertip present on nearly every dive in Kimbe and Eastern Fields — populations noticeably healthier than most of Southeast Asia. Hammerheads seasonal at deeper offshore sites.
- **Spinner dolphins**: Common throughout the Bismarck Sea; pods frequently approach liveaboards.
- **Manta rays**: Seasonal, primarily in Milne Bay and Witu Islands.
- **Dugongs**: Occasional sightings in Milne Bay's seagrass areas.
- **Schooling fish**: Bigeye jacks, barracuda, fusiliers constant at current-swept sites.

## Dive Conditions

- **Visibility**: Kimbe 15-30m (40m+ at offshore sites). Milne Bay 10-20m (river runoff and plankton — the richness feeds the macro life). Eastern Fields 30-50m.
- **Temperature**: 27-30C year-round. 3mm wetsuit standard; some prefer 5mm for repetitive multi-dive days.
- **Currents**: Mild to moderate at most Kimbe sites; strong at Inglis Shoal and Fathers Reefs. Milne Bay generally gentle.

## Season

- **Kimbe Bay**: Best Oct-May (calmer seas during northwest monsoon). Southeast trade winds (Jun-Sep) can make offshore sites rough.
- **Milne Bay**: Best Sep-Jan. Transition months offer calmest conditions and best visibility.
- **Eastern Fields**: Typically Nov-Apr when seas most manageable for open-water crossing.
- Water warm year-round — seasonality driven by sea state, not marine life migrations.

## Practical Info

- **Access**: International flights to Port Moresby (POM) via Brisbane, Cairns, Singapore, Manila, or Tokyo. Domestic Air Niugini to Hoskins (Kimbe) or Alotau (Milne Bay). Liveaboards arrange transfers.
- **Liveaboard operators**: MV FeBrina (Kimbe specialist, 12 guests, well-regarded for photography), MV Oceania (Milne Bay and Eastern Fields), Chertan. Trip lengths 7-10 nights. Expect USD $350-500+/night all-inclusive (nitrox typically extra).
- **Recompression chamber**: Nearest in Port Moresby. DAN membership and medevac insurance strongly recommended.
- **Safety**: Conservative profiles advisable. Malaria prophylaxis required. Medevac travel insurance essential.
- **Experience level**: Most sites suit Advanced OW+. Muck diving at Dinah's Beach accessible to Open Water. Offshore seamounts best for experienced current/blue-water divers.

## Conservation

- Customary sea tenure means local clans own and control reef access; several Kimbe communities have established tambu (no-take) zones.
- **The Nature Conservancy** has operated in Kimbe since the mid-1990s, supporting community marine management and original biodiversity surveys.
- **Mahonia Na Dari** ("Guardian of the Sea") Research and Conservation Centre provides marine education and reef monitoring support.
- **Conservation International** active in Milne Bay.
- No large formal marine parks, but the decentralized clan-based system effectively limits destructive fishing — contributing to the reef health that makes Papua New Guinea a strong recovery prospect.
