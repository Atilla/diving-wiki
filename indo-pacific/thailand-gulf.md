# Thailand — Gulf of Thailand (Koh Tao, Koh Phangan, Chumphon)

## Status

- The Gulf of Thailand was hit harder than the Andaman side: ~90% of corals bleached in 2024 vs 55% in the Andaman. Only 40-60% recovered (vs 60-70% Andaman).
- Gulf conditions are inherently tougher: warmer year-round (28-32C), lower visibility (5-15m typical, 20m+ rare), higher nutrient loads.
- **Koh Tao shallow reefs** (Japanese Gardens, Twins shallows, Mango Bay) have been in long-term decline from tourism pressure, sedimentation, and successive bleaching (2010, 2016, 2024). Japanese Gardens — once the showcase site — has been a shadow of its former self for years. The 2024 event made it worse.
- **Pinnacle sites remain the highlight**: Sail Rock (best site in the Gulf — schools of batfish, chevron barracuda, jacks, occasional whale sharks), Chumphon Pinnacle (bull sharks seasonal Dec-Mar but sightings increasingly unreliable), Southwest Pinnacle. These deeper sites weathered 2024 better.
- HTMS Sattakut wreck (18-30m) colonizing well with soft corals and fish aggregation.
- **Marine life**: whale sharks seasonal (Mar-May) but declining long-term frequency. Hawksbill and green turtles regularly seen. Bull shark encounters at Chumphon increasingly hit-or-miss.
- **Restoration**: Coral nurseries and artificial reef projects have been running since 2007, with measurable local gains. Koh Tao has one of the strongest grassroots marine conservation communities in Southeast Asia.
- **For experienced divers**: Koh Tao is primarily a training destination. Focus on Sail Rock, Chumphon Pinnacle, and Southwest Pinnacle. Do not expect the reef quality of the Andaman side.

## Key Dive Sites

#### Sail Rock

Best site in the Gulf — schools of batfish, chevron barracuda, jacks, occasional whale sharks. Deeper pinnacle site that weathered 2024 better.

#### Chumphon Pinnacle

Bull sharks seasonal Dec-Mar but sightings increasingly unreliable. Deeper site with better coral condition.

#### Southwest Pinnacle

Deeper pinnacle site that weathered 2024 better than shallow reefs.

#### HTMS Sattakut Wreck

18-30m. Colonizing well with soft corals and fish aggregation.

## Marine Life

- Whale sharks seasonal (Mar-May) but declining long-term frequency.
- Hawksbill and green turtles regularly seen.
- Bull shark encounters at Chumphon increasingly hit-or-miss.
- Schools of batfish, chevron barracuda, jacks at pinnacle sites.

## Dive Conditions

- **Visibility**: 5-15m typical, 20m+ rare. Best visibility 20-30m during Jan-Apr post-monsoon.
- **Temperature**: 27-30C year-round; can spike to 32-34C in Apr-May (bleaching risk window).
- **Currents**: Variable at pinnacle sites.

## Season

- **Best diving:** Jan-Apr (post-monsoon, best visibility 20-30m, calmest seas). Mar-May for whale shark encounters at Sail Rock (luck-dependent).
- **Good:** May-Sep (SW monsoon brings occasional windy days and short rain, but underwater conditions often very good; plankton attracts whale sharks).
- **Monsoon/avoid:** Oct-Dec (NE monsoon). Heavy rain arrives late October, continuing through December/January. Visibility drops to 5-10m from runoff. Seas can be rough. Dive shops stay open but conditions are poorest.
- **Chumphon bull sharks:** Dec-Mar (seasonal, increasingly unreliable — do not plan a trip around this).
- **Wetsuit:** rashguard or 3mm year-round; 5mm only if thermocline-sensitive.

## Practical Info

- **Access**: Fly Koh Samui + ferry to Koh Tao.
- **Experience level**: Primarily a training destination. Pinnacle sites suit experienced divers.

## Conservation

- Coral nurseries and artificial reef projects have been running since 2007, with measurable local gains. Koh Tao has one of the strongest grassroots marine conservation communities in Southeast Asia.
