# Bunaken National Park

## Status

**EXCELLENT — pristine walls, world-class biodiversity, stable condition**

- Bunaken National Park, established 1991, covers 89,065 hectares across five main islands (Bunaken, Manado Tua, Siladen, Mantehage, Nain) plus mainland coastal areas in the Sulawesi Sea north of Manado.
- The park's signature is **vertical wall diving** — sheer limestone and coral walls dropping 300m+ into the abyss, encrusted with gorgonians, whip corals, and soft corals in extraordinary density.
- Reef condition is generally good. Remote location and established park management provide some protection, though enforcement remains a challenge. Crown-of-thorns outbreaks have been reported periodically.
- Bangka Island, north of Bunaken, offers complementary diving — shallower reef slopes, canyons, and world-class macro on volcanic sand, extending the North Sulawesi diving experience.

## Key Dive Sites

### Bunaken Island Walls

#### Lekuan I, II, III

5–40m+. The flagship wall dives of Bunaken. Sheer drop-offs with dense gorgonians, barrel sponges, and whip corals. Green and hawksbill turtles are resident — encounters virtually guaranteed. Lekuan III offers the most relaxed conditions for photography.

#### Fukui

5–30m+. Wall and slope with exceptional macro alongside the wall drama. Spotted eagle rays at depth, mandarin fish in the rubble, Pontoh's pygmy seahorse on gorgonians. WAD Jun 2025: eagle rays at 40m, first mandarin fish sighting.

#### Alung Banua

5–25m. Wall dive with excellent nudibranch diversity. Sheltered from most currents — good for less experienced divers.

### Manado Tua

#### Mandolin Point

5–30m+. The turtle epicentre. WAD Jun 2025 counted 30-40 green turtles on a single dive — "Forget about Hawaii, Bali or the Philippines, Bunaken is my new turtle capital of the world." Schooling batfish, dramatic wall topography with the volcanic cone of Manado Tua rising above.

#### Negri

5–32m. Wall dive on Manado Tua's east side. Strong schools of batfish and fusiliers in the blue water column alongside the wall.

### Siladen Island

#### Pangalisang

5–34m+. Sandy slope transitioning to wall. Good mix of macro and wide-angle — nudibranchs on the slope, pelagics along the wall edge.

### Mainland (North Manado)

Sandy slopes with outstanding macro — WAD Jun 2025 logged 12 nudibranch species in two dives including flame flabellina, black-spotted trapania, orange-edged and jewel sap-sucking slugs. Baby frogfish on volcanic sand. Different character from the walls — more akin to muck diving.

### Bangka Island

#### Batu Goso

5–36m. Maze of steep slopes, caverns, and canyons. Blueberry gorgonians (rare blue soft coral), dense fish life, strong topography. WAD's favourite Bangka site.

#### Tanjung Husi

5–24m. Giant frogfish, baby whitetip sharks, pygmy seahorse on Muricella gorgonians. Good backlit coral photography opportunities.

Bangka's sandy sites also produce blue-ringed octopus, wonderpus, thorny seahorse, peacock mantis shrimp — serious macro alongside the reef diving.

### Special Dives

#### Black-water / Bonfire Diving

Available from Siladen and other resorts. Night dives in open water with lights attracting larval reef creatures — flounder larvae, mantis shrimp larvae, juvenile cephalopods. "Like drifting through space" (WAD, Jun 2025).

## Marine Life

- **Sea turtles**: The defining encounter. Green turtles in extraordinary abundance — 30-40+ on peak dives at Mandolin Point. Hawksbill turtles also common. Resting on ledges, swimming in pairs, rising through the blue.
- **Macro**: Pontoh's pygmy seahorse, mandarin fish (Fukui), frogfish (painted, giant, baby), nudibranchs in remarkable diversity, blue-ringed octopus and wonderpus (Bangka), thorny seahorse.
- **Eagle rays**: Spotted eagle rays along walls at depth.
- **Reef fish**: Massive schools of pyramid butterflyfish on the walls, batfish clouds, fusiliers, anthias. Dense fish life on healthy walls.
- **Pelagics**: Occasional reef sharks (whitetip, blacktip) along walls. Napoleon wrasse.
- **Night/black-water**: Larval-stage creatures, octopus, hunting morays.

## Dive Conditions

- **Visibility**: 20–40m on the walls, often exceptional. Can drop on mainland muck sites.
- **Temperature**: 27–30C year-round. 3mm wetsuit sufficient.
- **Currents**: Variable along the walls — gentle drift to moderate. Some sites can have stronger currents at certain tidal states. Walls provide reference points. Generally manageable for intermediates with guide support.
- **Experience level**: Many wall dives accessible to Open Water divers (wall-following at moderate depth). Deep walls and current-exposed sites require Advanced OW. Bangka macro accessible to all levels.

## Season

- **Year-round destination.** The Sulawesi Sea is relatively sheltered.
- **Dry season (May–Oct)**: Best conditions — calmer seas, better visibility, less rain. Peak Jul–Oct.
- **Wet season (Nov–Apr)**: More rain (often afternoon showers), slightly reduced visibility at times. Still very diveable — WAD dived in early June (transition period) with 28–30C water and good conditions despite some rain.
- **Combine with Lembeh**: Dry season (May–Nov) offers best conditions for both destinations.

## Practical Info

- **Access**: Fly to Manado (Sam Ratulangi Airport, MDC). Domestic flights from Jakarta, Bali, Makassar. Boat transfer to Bunaken Island ~45 min, Siladen ~15 min from Tiwoho harbour (~45 min drive from Manado). Bangka Island ~1.5 hrs by boat from Siladen.
- **Operators**: **Panorama Diving Bunaken** (personally recommended — owner Sven, established operation on Bunaken Island), Siladen Dive Resort (premium resort on Siladen Island, dedicated camera room), Coral Eye Resort (Bangka Island — former marine biology outpost turned eco-resort), Two Fish Divers, Froggies Divers.
- **Typical itinerary**: 4–5 days Bunaken/Siladen walls + 2–3 days Bangka + 2–3 days Lembeh = comprehensive North Sulawesi trip (10–14 days).
- **Recompression chamber**: Manado (near Bunaken). Accessible within 1–2 hours from most dive sites.

## Conservation

- **Bunaken National Park**: Established 1991, one of Indonesia's first marine parks. 89,065 hectares. Entrance fees fund park management.
- **Threats**: Blast fishing largely eliminated within the park but still occurs at boundaries. Crown-of-thorns starfish outbreaks periodically impact coral. Sedimentation from mainland development affects nearshore reefs. Tourism pressure managed through park fees and resort cooperation.
- **Bangka Island**: Community-based conservation efforts through dive resort partnerships.

## Trip Reports

- Jun 2025 (WAD blog): 4 days Bunaken/Siladen + 3 days Bangka as part of month-long Sulawesi crossing. Bunaken: "my new turtle capital of the world" — 30-40 turtles at Mandolin Point. Eagle rays and mandarin fish at Fukui. Pygmy seahorse. 12 nudibranch species on mainland dives. Bangka: blue-ringed octopus, wonderpus, blueberry gorgonians, pygmy seahorse. Water 28-30C, good visibility despite early wet-to-dry transition.
