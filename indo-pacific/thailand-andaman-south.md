# Thailand — Andaman Sea South (Koh Haa, Hin Daeng/Hin Muang, Phi Phi, Koh Rok, Koh Lipe)

## Status

- The southern Andaman emerges as a distinct and often superior alternative to the northern [Thailand — Andaman North](thailand-andaman-north.md) circuit.

## Key Dive Sites

### Hin Daeng / Hin Muang

Deep-water pinnacles **largely escaped the worst bleaching**. Hin Daeng covered in red soft corals, Hin Muang in purple. Jan 2026 trip report: "layers of red, orange and purple soft corals"; March 2026: reef "exploded with life." Walls drop to 60m+.

Premier manta ray aggregation site: 118 individual oceanic mantas documented since 2006 via citizen science. Peak sightings Feb-April. Designated an Important Shark and Ray Area (ISRA).

### Koh Haa

**Among the healthiest reefs in the region.** Sheltered lagoon with multiple numbered dive sites. Jan 2026 liveaboard report: "healthy coral structures" with turtles, reef sharks. Feb 2025 WAD trip report noted soft corals had suffered from the 2024 heat wave but showed signs of regrowth. Visibility up to 30m.

#### Koh Haa no.3 (Corner Lagoon)

Leopard sharks at depth, vibrant soft corals in red, purple, and orange, nudibranchs (mosaic halgerda, twin chromodoris).

#### Koh Haa no.5 (Cathedral)

Interconnected caverns with resident giant grouper. Strong macro: harlequin shrimps, ghost pipefish.

#### Koh Haa no.1 (Chimney)

Narrow vertical tunnel requiring impeccable buoyancy. Decorated walls.

### Koh Lipe / Tarutao National Marine Park

Thailand's oldest marine park (1974). **Among Thailand's healthiest reefs** due to remote location and lower tourism pressure.

#### Stonehenge

Vibrant pink and purple soft corals, closes annually for 3 months for regeneration.

#### 8 Mile Rock

Advanced deep pinnacle with hard corals, sea fans, huge schools of trevally/barracuda. Whale sharks possible Apr-Jun.

Bleaching affected shallow areas in 2024, but 60%+ recovered by early 2025. Rapid development and inadequate sewage on Koh Lipe itself is a concern for nearshore reefs.

### Phi Phi Islands

10 dive sites reopened April 2025 after nearly a year of closure due to bleaching. Up to 70% of coral fields bleached in 2024, but visible natural recovery.

#### Maya Bay

Conservation success — 50% of replanted coral surviving and growing, blacktip reef shark population rebounded from zero to ~60 individuals.

#### Koh Bida Nok & Koh Bida Nai

Blacktip sharks, tiger-tail seahorse, harlequin ghost pipefish, clouds of yellow snapper, gorgonians and harp corals. Described as "absolute must-dives from Koh Lanta" (WAD, Feb 2025).

#### Hin Bida

Leopard sharks "almost guaranteed" — resting on sandy bottom (WAD, Feb 2025). Scorpionfish, dense schools of snappers. Dentex hunting around the pinnacle.

### Koh Rok

Recovering well from 2024 (60%+). Described as "one of the prettiest and well-preserved for diving in Thailand." Extensive hard and soft coral on sandy slopes.

### Krabi Province Pinnacles

Peer-reviewed study confirmed only **13-18% bleaching** at underwater pinnacles — "high resilience and potential refuge." Depth, current exposure, and submerged structure provide natural thermal buffering.

A south Andaman liveaboard route (Koh Haa, Hin Daeng/Hin Muang, Koh Rok, potentially Koh Lipe) currently offers the best reef/pelagic combination outside the Similan zone. Combined south-to-north routes (adding Similans/Richelieu) run 7-8 nights.

## Marine Life

- **Manta rays**: 118 individual oceanic mantas documented at Hin Daeng/Hin Muang since 2006. Peak sightings Feb-April.
- **Reef sharks**: grey reef, whitetip, and blacktip reef sharks at current-swept sites.
- **Turtles**: hawksbill and green turtles at Koh Haa and other sites.
- **Schools**: trevally, barracuda at 8 Mile Rock and Hin Daeng.
- **Macro**: harlequin shrimps, ghost pipefish, nudibranchs at Koh Haa.
- **Whale sharks**: possible Apr-Jun at 8 Mile Rock.
- **Leopard sharks**: Near-guaranteed at Hin Bida (resting on sandy bottom). Also at Koh Haa no.3 at depth.
- **Tiger-tail seahorse, harlequin ghost pipefish**: At Koh Bida Nok — expanding the macro appeal of the south.

## Dive Conditions

| Sub-area | Visibility | Temperature | Currents | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Koh Haa** | 15-30m (consistently good) | 27-30C | Mild; sheltered lagoon/cavern sites | Calmer than Similans; all levels |
| **Hin Daeng / Hin Muang** | 10-25m (plankton-driven variation) | 27-30C | Moderate to strong; exposed open-ocean pinnacles | Most Similans-like — current attracts mantas |
| **Phi Phi** | 10-20m | 28-30C | Mild to moderate | Warmer and calmer; more macro-oriented |
| **Koh Rok** | 15-30m (among clearest in the south) | 28-30C | Gentle | Pristine hard coral gardens |
| **Koh Lipe / Adang-Rawi** | 10-25m | 28-30C | Mild to moderate | Southernmost Thai sites |

**Key differences from the northern Similans**: Southern sites are generally 1-2C warmer, calmer, and shallower. The north offers bigger granite boulder topography and stronger thermoclines. The south trades dramatic deep profiles for better coral coverage, more relaxed conditions, and Hin Daeng/Hin Muang as the pelagic focal point instead of Richelieu Rock.

## Season

- Same **Nov-Apr** as the north. Park closures mid-May to mid-Oct.
- Hin Daeng manta peak: **Feb-Apr**.
- Early season (Nov-Dec) calmest seas.

## Practical Info

- **Departure ports**: Most south itineraries from **Koh Lanta** or **Krabi**. Combined north-south from **Phuket** (Chalong Bay) or **Khao Lak**.
- **Liveaboard operators**: South-focused — MV Lapat (dedicated Koh Haa/Hin Daeng from Koh Lanta), MV Kepsub. Combined north-south — Deep Andaman Queen, MV Manta Queen fleet, SY Diva Andaman, MV Giamani. Premium — MV Halcyon, Sawasdee Fasai.
- **Typical itineraries**: South-only (3-4 nights): Koh Lanta -> Koh Haa -> Hin Daeng/Hin Muang -> Koh Rok -> return. Combined north-south (5-7 nights): Khao Lak -> Similans -> Koh Bon -> Richelieu -> transit south -> Koh Haa -> Hin Daeng -> end Koh Lanta.
- **Costs**: South-only 3-4 nights USD $500-1,000. Combined 5-7 nights $1,000-2,200. Day trips from Koh Lanta to Hin Daeng ~$130-190. Generally 10-20% cheaper than equivalent Similan-only trips.
- **Season**: Same **Nov-Apr** as the north. Park closures mid-May to mid-Oct. Hin Daeng manta peak: **Feb-Apr**. Early season (Nov-Dec) calmest seas.
- **Experience level**: Koh Haa and Koh Rok suitable for Open Water. Hin Daeng/Hin Muang require Advanced OW (depths to 50m+, open-ocean currents).
- **Recompression chamber**: SSS Phuket (same as north). Evacuation from Hin Daeng 3-4 hours by speedboat.

## Conservation

- **Coral restoration**: DMCR nurturing 60,000 coral colonies in nurseries nationally. Racha Yai 3D-printed coral structures (109 of 210 installed). Phuket Coral Cryobank operational since June 2025 — preserving coral larvae at -196C as "genetic insurance."
