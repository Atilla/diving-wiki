# Socorro / Revillagigedo

## Status

- Minimal coral due to volcanic landscape and cooler temps. This is 100% a megafauna destination.
- Giant oceanic mantas, silky/Galapagos/hammerhead/whale sharks, dolphins, humpback whales.
- June 2025 guest: *"it is a pearl! Unbelievable how healthy the biodiversity is."*
- **One of very few destinations completely uncompromised by bleaching.** Season: Nov-May.

## Key Dive Sites

### Socorro Island

#### Cabo Pearce

15–40m along reef shelf, dropping to 1,000m+. The signature site for giant oceanic manta encounters — mantas cruise cleaning stations along the ridge. Strong currents, drift dive. Hammerheads, Galapagos sharks, whitetip reef sharks, occasional whale sharks. Volcanic boulder fields with scattered hard coral.

#### Punta Tosca

5–30m+, sloping volcanic wall. The most reliable site for "friendly manta" behavior — mantas actively seek diver interaction, hovering overhead for bubble contact for minutes at a time. Lower current than Cabo Pearce. Whitetip reef shark aggregations in sandy channels. Bottlenose dolphin encounters.

### San Benedicto Island

#### The Boiler

3–30m+. Submerged volcanic pinnacle — widely considered the archipelago's premier site. Mantas circle the pinnacle reliably; 4–8 individuals per dive on good days. Galapagos sharks, silky sharks, large schools of jacks resident. Summit has encrusting sponges and hydroids rather than coral. Surge can be significant in shallows.

#### The Canyon

12–25m. Sheltered leeward site, volcanic ridges forming a channel. Calmer conditions — good check-dive or night-dive location. Whitetip reef sharks, green morays, large lobsters, occasional mantas.

### Roca Partida

The entire site is a single vertical rock pinnacle rising from abyssal depths, barely 30m long at the surface. Walls to 30–40m. Famous for "wall of sharks" — aggregations of 50–100+ whitetip reef sharks, Galapagos sharks, silky sharks, schooling hammerheads in the blue. Whale sharks Nov–Dec and Apr–May. Tiger sharks sighted with increasing frequency (~20–30% of trips). Most exposed site: strong currents, surge, blue-water conditions — advanced only.

### Clarion Island

Most remote island (~400km further southwest). Only a handful of operators include it on extended 10–12 day itineraries. Exploratory diving with silky sharks and mantas. Long transit limits inclusion.

## Marine Life

- **Giant oceanic mantas**: Resident population estimated at 350–500 individually photo-identified animals. Their behavior of actively approaching divers for bubble contact is essentially unique to this population globally. Near-guaranteed at The Boiler and Punta Tosca Nov–May. Wingspans reach 5–6m.
- **Sharks**: Whitetip reef sharks omnipresent. Galapagos sharks (2–3m) at deeper ledges. Silky sharks in open water. Hammerheads school at Roca Partida and Cabo Pearce — ~60–70% encounter rate during peak. Whale sharks seasonal (Nov–Dec, Apr–May). Tiger sharks increasingly reported at Roca Partida.
- **Humpback whales**: Present Jan–Mar. Mothers with calves in warm waters. Singing males audible on nearly every Feb dive; surface sightings between dives common. In-water encounters incidental, not structured.
- **Bottlenose dolphins**: Resident population at Punta Tosca and Cabo Pearce. Sustained, curious interactions — circling, eye contact, lingering. Many returning divers cite these encounters as more memorable than the mantas.

## Dive Conditions

- **Visibility**: 15–30m typical. Best (25–30m) Nov–Dec and Apr–May. Mid-season (Jan–Feb) can drop to 12–20m with plankton blooms.
- **Temperature**: 21–26C. Nov–Dec: 24–26C. Jan–Mar can drop to 21–23C — colder than many expect. Thermoclines cut 3–4C below 25m. 7mm wetsuit or drysuit not unreasonable in peak winter. Apr–May warms to 24–26C.
- **Currents and surge**: Moderate to strong. Roca Partida and Cabo Pearce regularly 2–3 knots. All sites intermediate-to-advanced.

## Season

| Period | Highlights | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| **Nov–Dec** | Warm water, best visibility, whale sharks arriving, mantas active | 24–26C, 25–30m vis |
| **Jan–Mar** | Humpback whales, peak hammerhead aggregations, dolphins very active | 21–24C, 15–25m vis, thermoclines |
| **Apr–May** | Warming water, whale sharks returning, mantas still strong, fewer boats | 24–26C, 20–30m vis |

Jan–Mar considered overall peak for diversity of encounters despite cooler water.

## Practical Info

- **Departure**: All liveaboards from Cabo San Lucas, Baja California Sur. ~22–26 hours transit each way.
- **Trip length**: 8–9 days total (4–5 full diving days, 3–4 dives/day). Extended Clarion itineraries 10–12 days.
- **Operators**: Nautilus Explorer, Solmar V, Rocio del Mar, Quino el Guardian, Valentina, Vortex. Fleet size limited by park permits.
- **Cost**: USD $3,500–$5,500 per person. Nitrox widely available (sometimes included, sometimes $100–150 extra).
- **Experience level**: Minimum Advanced Open Water and 50–100 logged dives. Strong current experience highly recommended.
- **Recompression chamber**: Nearest in Cabo San Lucas (Hospiten hospital) — 22–26 hours away by boat. DAN insurance and conservative profiles strongly advised.

## Conservation

- **UNESCO World Heritage Site** (2016). **Revillagigedo National Park** (2017) — one of the largest fully protected marine reserves in North America (~148,000 km2). All extractive fishing banned. Previously permitted longlining was devastating shark populations.
- **Diver regulations**: No gloves, no spearfishing, no collecting. Operators must hold CONANP permits. Number of boats in park limited at any time. Park fee typically included in trip cost.
- **Enforcement**: Mexico's navy (SEMAR) maintains a garrison on Socorro and conducts regular patrols. Illegal longlining incursions have decreased significantly since 2017 designation. Overall trajectory for shark and manta populations appears positive.
