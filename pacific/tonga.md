# Tonga

## Status

- One of the very few places in the world where in-water swimming with humpback whales is legal and commercially operated. Whales migrate from Antarctica to Tongan waters to breed and calve July–October.
- **Encounters are snorkel-based** (no scuba — bubbles disturb the whales). Operators run small-boat excursions with groups of 4–8 snorkelers. Mothers and calves resting near the surface are the highlight. Visibility 25–40m in open water. Success rates are high — 80–90% of multi-day trips report whale encounters during peak months.
- **Vava'u** is the main whale-swimming hub. **Ha'apai** offers fewer operators but better reef diving and the South Pacific's biggest known coral arch (Arch of Ofolanga).
- Reef health suffered from Cyclone Harold (2020) and the **Hunga Tonga eruption/tsunami (January 2022)**, but 2024 recovery reports indicate coral regrowth is progressing.

## Practical Info

- **Access**: Fly to Tongatapu (Nuku'alofa) then domestic flight to Vava'u (~1 hour) or Ha'apai. International flights from Auckland, Sydney, Fiji.
- **Operators**: Deep Blue Diving (Vava'u — whale swims + reef diving), Whale Discoveries (dedicated whale-swim operator), Beluga Diving (Ha'apai). Resort-based and day-boat diving.
- **Costs**: Whale swim day trips ~USD $250–350/person. Reef diving $100–150 for 2-tank. Accommodation from budget ($40/night) to mid-range ($150–300/night).
- **Season**: **July–October** for whales (peak Aug–Sep). Reef diving year-round, best May–November.
- **Temperature:** 23–28C. Coolest Jul–Sep (~23–25C), warmest Jan–Mar (~27–28C). 3mm for warm season; 5mm recommended during whale season (cooler water).
- **Cyclone risk:** Dec–Apr (peak Jan–Mar). Not a major cyclone corridor but Cyclone Harold (2020) and the Hunga Tonga eruption (2022) demonstrate vulnerability.
- **Note**: Scuba diving WITH whales is prohibited — but reef diving is available year-round alongside whale snorkeling trips.
