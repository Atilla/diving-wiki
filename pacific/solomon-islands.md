# Solomon Islands

## Status

- Over 5,700 km of reef across a double chain of six major islands and hundreds of smaller ones. One of the Pacific's last true frontier diving destinations — reef health in the Solomons remains excellent by global standards, benefiting from remoteness and low population pressure.
- **Iron Bottom Sound** (the strait between Guadalcanal, Savo, and Florida Islands) contains dozens of WWII wrecks from the 1942–43 naval battles — the name refers to the sheer number of ships and aircraft on the seabed.
- **Marovo Lagoon**: the world's largest double-barrier enclosed saltwater lagoon, a UNESCO tentative site. Cathedral-like coral formations, pristine walls.
- 2024 trip reports consistently describe "pristine soft corals that rival or exceed Raja Ampat" with far fewer divers.

## Key Dive Sites

### Iron Bottom Sound (Guadalcanal/Florida Islands)

Dozens of WWII wrecks — destroyers, transports, aircraft — at depths from 15m to beyond recreational limits. The **I-1 Japanese submarine** (23m) is a standout. **Bonegi I and Bonegi II** transports are shore-accessible from Guadalcanal. Many deeper wrecks remain largely uncharted — growing interest from technical divers.

Reef diving around the Florida Islands is excellent, with healthy hard and soft corals.

### Marovo Lagoon (New Georgia)

Pristine hard coral gardens, dramatic walls on the outer barrier reef, sheltered lagoon diving with high visibility. Uepi Island Resort is the main dive base.

### Russell Islands

Excellent wall diving, pelagic encounters, very few divers. Liveaboard access primarily.

### Mary Island (south of Malaita)

Uninhabited, pristine, liveaboard-only access. Walls, reef sharks, untouched coral.

## Marine Life

- Reef sharks (grey reef, whitetip, blacktip) present at outer sites. Fish diversity benefits from Coral Triangle adjacency.
- Pelagic encounters include barracuda schools, eagle rays, occasional hammerheads at deeper offshore sites.
- Turtles (green and hawksbill) common. Manta rays seasonal at some outer passages.
- Soft coral density on outer reefs rivals the best Indo-Pacific sites.

## Dive Conditions

- **Visibility**: 15–40m depending on site and season. Outer reefs and Iron Bottom Sound sites typically 20–30m+.
- **Temperature**: 27–30C year-round. 3mm sufficient.
- **Currents**: Mild to moderate at most sites. Some outer reef passages produce stronger flow.

## Practical Info

- **Access**: Fly to Honiara (Henderson International Airport) via Brisbane (Solomon Airlines) or Fiji. Infrastructure outside Honiara is basic.
- **Liveaboard operators**: **MV Bilikiki** (since 1989, now under Emperor Divers management since 2025) — 7- and 10-night itineraries covering Florida Islands, Russell Islands, Marovo Lagoon, Iron Bottom Sound. **Solomons PNG Master** (Master Liveaboards) rotates seasonally between Solomons and Papua New Guinea.
- **Land-based**: Uepi Island Resort (Marovo Lagoon). Limited shore-based diving from Honiara.
- **Costs**: Liveaboard 7–10 nights USD $4,500–7,500 depending on vessel and cabin. Land-based at Uepi considerably less.
- **Season**: March–December. Best conditions October–December (transitional season — calmer seas, good visibility).
- **Recompression chamber**: Limited — DAN insurance and conservative profiles essential.
- **Experience level**: Most sites suit Advanced Open Water. Wreck penetration requires appropriate certification. Some outer sites advanced due to currents.
