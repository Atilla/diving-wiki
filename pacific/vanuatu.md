# Vanuatu

## Status

- The **SS President Coolidge** in Espiritu Santo is the world's largest easily accessible wreck dive — a 199m (654 ft) luxury liner converted to troop transport that sank in 1942 after hitting friendly mines. It lies at 20–70m, making parts accessible to recreational divers while offering deep technical penetration. **Shore entry** — you walk in from the beach.
- **Million Dollar Point** nearby is a surreal site where US forces dumped millions of dollars of military equipment (trucks, jeeps, bulldozers, Coca-Cola bottles) into the sea rather than leave it for locals — now a coral-encrusted reef.

## Key Dive Sites

#### SS President Coolidge

The promenade deck at ~25–35m is accessible to advanced open water divers. "The Lady" (a large porcelain artwork) at ~40m and "The Lady and the Unicorn" are signature penetration targets. The ship is slowly deteriorating — some deck sections have collapsed — but remains spectacular.

#### Million Dollar Point

3–30m. Equipment dump cascading down a slope. Trucks, jeeps, bulldozers, forklifts, ammunition, Coca-Cola crates, all encrusted with coral. An eerie, photogenic site.

#### Blue holes

Freshwater/saltwater cave systems — Nanda Blue Hole, Matevulu Blue Hole. Remarkable clarity.

Reef diving around Efate (Port Vila) and Santo is decent but not the primary draw.

## Dive Conditions

- **Visibility:** 15–30m. Best May–Oct. Can drop after heavy rain in wet season.
- **Temperature:** 24–29C. Coolest Jul–Aug (~24–26C), warmest Jan–Apr (~28–29C). 3mm sufficient year-round; 5mm for deeper Coolidge dives in winter.
- **Coolidge wreck:** diveable year-round (sheltered location in Second Channel, Santo). Weather rarely prevents Coolidge dives even in wet season.

## Season

- **Best:** May–Oct (dry season — cooler, lower humidity, calmer seas, best visibility). Apr marks the transition into peak diving.
- **Cyclone season: Nov–Apr (peak late Dec–early Apr).** Vanuatu is cyclone-prone — this is a real risk, not theoretical. Tropical storms can shut down diving for days and damage infrastructure. Travel insurance with cyclone coverage essential for wet-season bookings.

## Practical Info

- **Access**: International flights to Port Vila (VLI) from Australia, New Zealand, Fiji, New Caledonia. Domestic flight to Luganville/Santo (~50 min) for the Coolidge.
- **Operators**: Santo Island Dive (Allan Power Dive Tours legacy — the original Coolidge specialists), Aore Adventure Divers. Big Blue Vanuatu (Efate).
- **Costs**: Coolidge dives ~USD $100–150/dive. Accommodation in Luganville budget-friendly ($50–150/night).
- **No liveaboard scene** — resort/day-boat operations.
- **Experience level**: Coolidge recreational areas require Advanced Open Water. Deep penetration is technical-level.
