# Mauritius

## Status

**FAIR TO POOR — pleasant but not a serious dive destination; Wakashio legacy**

- Nearly encircled by ~150 km of fringing reef creating a 243 km2 lagoon system. Reef condition has been declining for decades from chronic stress: agricultural runoff (sugar cane), sewage, sedimentation, overfishing, and tourism pressure.
- **2024 bleaching**: Alert Level 1-2 from NOAA during the 2023-2024 southern hemisphere summer. Mascarene region benefits from cooler South Equatorial Current influence, so impact was likely less catastrophic than Seychelles inner islands or northern Maldives. However, reefs were already weakened entering the event from cumulative stress.

### MV Wakashio oil spill (2020) — ongoing impact

- ~1,000 tonnes of fuel oil spilled near Pointe d'Esny in August 2020. Localized coral mortality from direct oil contact and dispersant chemicals. Persistent hydrocarbon contamination in sediments documented through 2023. Bioaccumulation in fish and invertebrates confirmed.
- Blue Bay Marine Park was partially shielded but affected. The park was already in decline — coral cover dropped from historical 50-60% to 20-30% — and the spill added acute stress to chronic degradation.

## Key Dive Sites

#### Cathedral (Flic-en-Flac)

Mauritius's signature dive — caverns/swim-throughs at 18-28m on the outer reef slope. Appeal is geological (volcanic formations), not coral. Soft corals on walls, lionfish, morays, lobsters. Relatively resilient to bleaching. Visibility 15-25m.

#### Coin de Mire / Flat Island (northern islets)

Wall diving, better water quality than mainland, reef sharks more regularly seen. The strongest diving in the north.

#### Whale Rock, Colorado (west coast)

Offshore pinnacle/drift sites with pelagic encounters — barracuda, trevally, occasional reef sharks. Better coral than inshore sites.

#### Lagoon sites

Generally turbid and degraded. Not recommended for experienced divers.

### Rodrigues Island (~650 km east)

Healthier reefs than mainland Mauritius due to lower population density, less development, and less runoff. Lagoon roughly twice the island's area — one of the largest in the WIO. Community-managed MPAs and octopus fishery closures are working.

Very limited dive infrastructure. A niche option for adventurous divers.

## Marine Life

- **Dolphins**: spinner and bottlenose resident along the west coast (Tamarin Bay). Major attraction but extremely heavy boat pressure — behavioral disruption documented.
- Reef sharks: whitetip and occasional blacktip at outer sites, but populations significantly depleted by fishing. Encounters not reliable.
- Humpback whales (Jun-Nov) and sperm whales (year-round in offshore canyons). Turtle encounters modest around mainland, better in Rodrigues.
- Fish biomass notably lower than less-fished Indian Ocean locations.

## Dive Conditions

- **Visibility**: 12-35m depending on season and site. Best winter/dry season; worst after cyclone-season rains.
- **Temperature:** 22–28C. Coolest Jul–Aug (~22–24C), warmest Feb–Mar (~27–28C). 3mm warm season; 5mm recommended Jun–Sep.
- **No liveaboard industry.** The continuous fringing reef doesn't reward multi-day boat exploration — most sites reachable from 2-3 shore bases. All diving is shore-based from ~50 dive centers concentrated on the west and north coasts.
- **Mauritius is a combined holiday + diving destination, not a dedicated dive trip.** An experienced diver would choose Seychelles outer islands, Pemba, or the Maldives over Mauritius for underwater quality alone.

## Season

- **Best:** Oct–Dec and Mar–Apr (warm water, good visibility 20–35m, pre/post-cyclone calm).
- **Cyclone season/avoid: Jan–Mar.** Peak Jan–Feb. Torrential rain, strong winds, frequent dive cancellations, reduced visibility (12–15m). This is a real operational risk, not a footnote.
- **Winter (Jun–Sep):** cooler, drier, good visibility (20–30m). Humpback whales Jun–Nov. Sperm whales year-round offshore. Best season for whale encounters but cooler water.
- **Dolphins (Tamarin Bay):** year-round but heavy boat pressure has disrupted behavior.

## Practical Info

- **Access**: Fly to Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam International Airport (MRU). Direct flights from Europe, Dubai, and regional hubs.
- **Operators**: ~50 dive centers on the west and north coasts.
- **No liveaboard industry.** Shore-based diving only.
