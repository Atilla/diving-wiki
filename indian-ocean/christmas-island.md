# Christmas Island

## Status

**GOOD — unique ecological spectacle; whale sharks linked to red crab spawning**

- An Australian external territory in the Indian Ocean, 350 km south of Java. Famous for the terrestrial red crab migration (Oct–Dec), but the underwater connection is the draw for divers: female red crabs release eggs into the ocean, creating massive larval clouds that attract plankton feeders including **whale sharks** (Nov–Apr) and manta rays.
- Dramatic wall diving off a steep volcanic island — seabed drops to 4,000m+ within a few kilometres of shore. Healthy hard and soft corals, minimal human impact, nutrient-rich upwelling waters. Large pelagics patrol the walls — silvertip sharks, occasional hammerheads, thresher sharks reported.
- **Flying Fish Cove** may be one of the world's best shore dives.

## Key Dive Sites

- **Flying Fish Cove**: Possibly one of the world's best shore dives.
- Dramatic wall diving with walls dropping to 4,000m+ within kilometres of shore.
- Healthy hard and soft corals with minimal human impact.

## Marine Life

- **Whale sharks**: Nov-Apr, linked to red crab egg release.
- **Manta rays**: Attracted by red crab larvae.
- **Sharks**: Silvertip sharks, occasional hammerheads, thresher sharks reported on the walls.
- Nutrient-rich upwelling waters support high biodiversity.

## Dive Conditions

- **Temperature:** 26–29C year-round. Minimal seasonal variation. 3mm or rashguard sufficient.
- **Visibility**: 20-40m.
- **Currents**: Variable along walls.

## Season

- **Whale sharks**: Nov–Apr (linked to red crab egg release). Best overall Nov–Apr for pelagic encounters. Diveable year-round.

## Practical Info

- **Operators**: Christmas Island Wet 'n' Dry Adventures, Extra Divers Christmas Island. Small operations, limited capacity.
- **Access**: Flights from Perth (seasonal Virgin Australia) or via Jakarta. Australian territory — normal visa rules apply. One main settlement. Accommodation limited ($100–250 AUD/night).
- **Combine with Cocos (Keeling) Islands** (flights via Christmas Island) — Indian Ocean Experiences offers packages covering both. Cocos has a single dive operator (Cocos Dive) with pristine atoll reefs, spectacular manta encounters, and 15,000+ green turtles in the lagoon — but extremely limited capacity.
