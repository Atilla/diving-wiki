# Oman

## Status

**HIDDEN GEM — naturally protected; upwelling held during 2024 — but NOT VIABLE as of April 2026 due to Iran-US conflict**

- Arabian Sea monsoon upwelling (khareef, Jun-Sep) drops sea surface temperatures (SSTs) from ~28C to 18-22C along the Dhofar coast — one of the most intense upwelling systems in the world. **This protection appears to have held during the 2023-2024 global bleaching event.** Daymaniyat Islands and Muscat-area reefs escaped mass bleaching; Degree Heating Weeks (DHW) remained at or below Alert Level 1.

## Key Dive Sites

#### Daymaniyat Islands Nature Reserve — the flagship

Nine uninhabited islands, Oman's healthiest reef system. Hard coral cover 40-70% on best sites — exceptional by global standards. Dense Acropora table and staghorn gardens in 3-8m are a hallmark. Green turtles almost guaranteed every dive, blacktip reef sharks, Arabian angelfish (endemic), hammerheads at deeper sites.

Best season Oct-Mar. Visibility 8-20m (nutrient-rich water, not Red Sea clarity).

#### Musandam Peninsula

Dramatic limestone fjords ("Norway of Arabia"). Steep walls, swim-throughs, soft corals. Coral inside the fjords is more variable (10-30% cover, warmer restricted water). Outside the fjords — headlands and islands exposed to Strait of Hormuz currents — significantly better. Lima Rock and Telegraph Island best sites.

Blacktip reef sharks common, Indo-Pacific humpback dolphins in the khors, cuttlefish (Pharaoh cuttlefish — Oman may be one of the best places in the world for reliable quality encounters).

#### Dhofar Coast (Salalah)

Prime whale shark zone (Oct-Mar, peak Nov-Jan), driven by post-upwelling plankton blooms. Non-migratory Arabian Sea humpback whale population (<100 individuals, critically important). Mobula rays, spinner dolphins.

Kelp-like macroalgae grow during upwelling — almost unique in the Arabian region. Reefs have distinct East African/Somali biogeographic affinity.

#### Hallaniyat Islands

Five islands off Dhofar coast, genuinely unexplored. Minimal survey data but high fish biomass from minimal fishing pressure. Oman's true frontier diving — requires dedicated charter.

- **Upwelling reliability under climate change**: the monsoon-driven system has persisted for millions of years and some models suggest it could intensify with warming. But background deep ocean warming means upwelled water is gradually getting warmer. Gulf of Oman (Daymaniyat) gets less upwelling benefit than Dhofar.
- **Liveaboards do exist** (correction — previously listed as none): **Oman Aggressor** (22 divers, 11 staterooms, luxury), **Oman Explorer / Samana Explorer** (similar standard), and **MV Vision** (new build 2025, 29m, 12 guests, Musandam-focused). Northern itineraries (Daymaniyat, Musandam) run Jun–Nov; southern itineraries (Hallaniyat Islands) Dec–Apr. Season Sep–May overall. Muscat also has good shore-based operators.
- **Best strategy (normal conditions)**: combine Muscat/Daymaniyat (3-4 days) with Musandam (2-3 days). Add Dhofar in Oct-Nov for whale sharks.

## Marine Life

- **Whale sharks**: Dhofar coast Oct-Mar (peak Nov-Jan).
- **Sharks**: Blacktip reef sharks common; hammerheads at deeper Daymaniyat sites.
- **Cuttlefish**: Pharaoh cuttlefish at Musandam — among the world's most reliable encounters.
- **Humpback whales**: Non-migratory Arabian Sea population (<100 individuals) at Dhofar.
- **Turtles**: Green turtles nearly guaranteed at Daymaniyat.

## Dive Conditions

- **Visibility**: 8-20m (nutrient-rich water, not Red Sea clarity).
- **Temperature**: Daymaniyat 22-28C; Dhofar 18-22C during upwelling, warming to 25-28C Oct-Mar.
- **Currents**: Variable; strong in Musandam and at exposed sites.

## Season

- **Daymaniyat**: Oct-Mar best.
- **Musandam**: Year-round but best Oct-May.
- **Dhofar whale sharks**: Oct-Mar (peak Nov-Jan).
- **Liveaboard season**: Sep-May overall.

### April 2026: Iran-US conflict — Oman diving not viable

- US/Israeli strikes on Iran began Feb 28, 2026. Iran effectively closed the Strait of Hormuz on March 2 (traffic collapsed from ~150 ships/day to 4–5).
- **Drone strikes hit Duqm and Salalah** in March 2026 — a fuel storage tank at Duqm was damaged. These are not border areas; Salalah is Oman's second city and the gateway to Dhofar/Hallaniyat diving.
- **Musandam Peninsula directly borders the Strait of Hormuz.** Khasab tourism disrupted; cruise ships pulled out of the Persian Gulf entirely. MV Vision's operating area is directly in the conflict zone.
- US travel advisory raised to **Level 3 (Reconsider Travel)** on March 3–4, 2026. US embassy ordered evacuation as recently as April 9, even after a ceasefire was announced.
- Oman remains officially neutral and cities like Muscat and Nizwa function, but the physical proximity to active hostilities makes dive tourism untenable — particularly Musandam and Dhofar/Salalah.
- **A ceasefire has been announced but the situation remains volatile.** Monitor for stabilization before planning. Daymaniyat (nearest Muscat, furthest from conflict zones) would likely be the first area to become viable again.

## Practical Info

- **Access**: Fly to Muscat (MCT). International hub with connections worldwide.
- **Liveaboard operators**: Oman Aggressor, Oman Explorer / Samana Explorer, MV Vision (Musandam-focused).
- **Best strategy (normal conditions)**: Combine Muscat/Daymaniyat (3-4 days) with Musandam (2-3 days). Add Dhofar in Oct-Nov for whale sharks.
