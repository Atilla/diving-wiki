# Madagascar

## Status

**VARIABLE — whale sharks and Mitsio excellent; reef health mixed; infrastructure basic**

- Reef conditions vary dramatically by region. Best diving at the Mitsio Archipelago (north of Nosy Be) — remote granite/basalt islands with less fishing pressure, good-to-very-good coral cover. Worst at the historically magnificent Grand Reef of Toliara (southwest), degraded from >50% coral to <20% by deforestation sediment and destructive fishing.
- **Sedimentation from deforestation is the single greatest threat.** Madagascar has lost ~44% of forest cover since the 1950s. Rivers carry massive sediment loads visible from space.

## Key Dive Sites

#### Nosy Be — the main dive hub

Reefs around Nosy Be proper are moderately degraded (sedimentation, overfishing, anchor damage). Better diving at satellite islands: Nosy Tanikely (marine reserve, 30-45% coral cover), Nosy Sakatia, Nosy Komba.

- **Whale sharks**: seasonally reliable Sep-Dec (peak Oct-Nov), predominantly juveniles. One of the more reliable aggregation sites in the Indian Ocean.
- Green and hawksbill turtles regular. Reef sharks present but depleted. Good macro (nudibranchs, leaf scorpionfish, pipefish, pygmy seahorses reported).

#### Mitsio Archipelago

Walls, pinnacles, current-swept channels with better coral and larger pelagics. Reached by liveaboard or long day-trip from Nosy Be. **The standout diving in Madagascar** when conditions allow.

#### SW Mahafaly Seascape

Identified as potential climate refugium — subtropical position (23-25S) means 1-3C cooler baseline SSTs. Lower historical bleaching severity. But reefs under severe local stress from destructive fishing and coral mining for lime. Diving infrastructure essentially nonexistent.

## Marine Life

- **Whale sharks**: Sep-Dec (peak Oct-Nov) around Nosy Be. Plankton-driven, predominantly juveniles.
- **Humpback whales**: Jun-Sep (breeding season).
- **Turtles**: Green and hawksbill regular.
- **Sharks**: Present but depleted by fishing.
- **Macro**: Nudibranchs, leaf scorpionfish, pipefish, pygmy seahorses.

## Dive Conditions

- **Temperature:** 23–30C. Coolest Jul–Aug (~23–25C), warmest Jan–Mar (~28–30C). 3mm warm season; 5mm recommended Jun–Sep.
- **Visibility:** 15–30m. Best Sep–Nov. Worst Jan–Mar (sedimentation from wet-season runoff).

## Season

- **Best overall:** Sep–Dec (post-winter warming, whale sharks arriving, humpback whales departing, calm seas, improving visibility).
- **Whale sharks:** Sep–Dec (peak Oct–Nov) around Nosy Be. Plankton-driven, predominantly juveniles.
- **Humpback whales:** Jun–Sep (breeding season). Combined with whale sharks in Sep creates a brief overlap window.
- **Mitsio access:** weather-dependent. Most accessible mid-Mar to mid-Dec. Rough seas Jan–Feb can prevent long day-trips.
- **Cyclone season/avoid:** Mid-Jan to mid-Mar. Cyclonic risks, heavy rain, reduced visibility from runoff (the worst time for sedimentation impact). Infrastructure disruptions possible.
- **Dry season:** Apr–Oct. Best air comfort, least rain. Jul–Sep coolest water but best for humpback whales.

- **For the right diver**: whale shark season at Nosy Be is a genuine highlight. Mitsio delivers when accessible. The combination of diving + terrestrial biodiversity (lemurs, chameleons, baobabs) is unmatched. But infrastructure is basic, logistics unreliable, and reef quality below the Indian Ocean's best.

## Conservation

- **Blue Ventures**: pioneered Locally Managed Marine Areas (LMMAs) — now 200+ communities along the coast. Octopus closure model has been remarkably successful. Published reef recovery data in managed no-take zones.
- **MIHARI Network**: one of the largest community marine conservation networks in the world.

## Practical Info

- **No established liveaboard operations.** Occasional expedition vessels offer seasonal trips. This is fundamentally a shore-based destination.
- **Safety concern**: no functioning hyperbaric chamber on Nosy Be or in northern Madagascar. Nearest in Reunion Island. Medevac insurance essential.
- **Access**: Fly to Nosy Be via Antananarivo.
