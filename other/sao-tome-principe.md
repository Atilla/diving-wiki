# Sao Tome and Principe

## Status

- STP does **not have significant coral reef systems**. The underwater landscape is volcanic — basalt boulders, lava formations, walls, pinnacles, and seamounts with scattered hard coral colonies on rock substrate. This is a tropical rocky reef ecosystem, not a coral reef ecosystem. Divers expecting Indo-Pacific or Caribbean coral gardens will not find them.
- **One of the least-surveyed tropical island marine environments in the world.** No systematic coral monitoring, no GCRMN stations, no published bleaching data from the 2023-2024 global event. The scientific data gap is itself a key finding.

## Key Dive Sites

### Principe Island — the better diving

UNESCO Biosphere Reserve (2012). Cleaner water, more intact marine habitats, better fish biomass than Sao Tome.

#### Ilheu Bom Bom

Most famous dive area — volcanic rock formations, good fish life, occasional pelagics.

#### Pedra Gale

Rocky pinnacle with fish aggregations and pelagic potential.

#### Tinhosas Islands

Offshore seabird nesting site with potentially the best diving in the archipelago — large fish schools, sharks, pelagics. But difficult to access (weather-dependent, distance offshore).

Biosphere Reserve marine zones designated but enforcement minimal.

### Sao Tome Island

More dive infrastructure (larger island, capital city) but more impacted coastal waters. Southern and western coasts less affected by river runoff.

#### Ilheu das Rolas

Small island straddling the equator. Dive sites around the islet. Equator crossing as novelty.

## Marine Life

- **Sea turtles**: critically important nesting site — green, hawksbill, olive ridley, and leatherback. Nesting season Sep-Mar. One of the most significant nesting areas in the Gulf of Guinea. Turtle encounters while diving are a genuine highlight.
- **Humpback whales**: Gulf of Guinea breeding population (B1 stock), present Jul-Oct/Nov. Boat-based watching, not in-water encounters.
- **Dolphins**: bottlenose, Atlantic spotted, spinner, pantropical spotted. Regular boat sightings.
- **Sharks**: depleted by overfishing. Not a shark diving destination. Occasional nurse sharks, blacktips.
- **Unique biogeography**: tropical Eastern Atlantic species that divers won't see in Indo-Pacific or Caribbean — Guinea parrotfish, African sergeant major, Eastern Atlantic morays and wrasses. A fish-ID enthusiast would find species unavailable on any standard dive itinerary.

## Dive Conditions

- **Visibility: 5-25m** (highly variable). Nutrient-rich Gulf of Guinea waters support plankton, reducing clarity. Best during dry season (Jun-Sep). Notably lower than Red Sea, Maldives, or Caribbean averages.
- **Temperature: 26-30C** year-round, but upwelling (Jun-Sep) can drop to 23-25C at depth.
- **Currents**: can be strong and unpredictable around headlands and offshore sites. Experienced divers only at many sites.

## Practical Info

### Dive infrastructure — small but functional on Sao Tome

- **Sao Tome has several established dive centers** with proper equipment and PADI certification:
  - **Dive Tribe**: two bases — one at Hotel Pestana Sao Tome (north), one at Hotel Pestana Equador on Ilheu das Rolas (south/equator). Mares diving center with brand new equipment, BAUER filling stations with Securus System. PADI and SSI certified. Most sites 5-10 minutes from shore.
  - **Atlantic Diving Center**: at Resort Club Santana, 20 min from airport. PADI dive center with "last generation equipment," scuba equipment rental, BAUER compressor with nitrox membrane filling system, equipment service and repair, onsite training pool and classroom. Multi-language instruction. Open Water, Advanced, and **Nitrox courses available**. Dec 2024 review: "professional and personalized experience, highly recommended."
  - **Dive Sao Tome**: operates from Pestana hotel. Positive recent reviews — well-organized and professional.
- **Principe Island**: diving is **not currently available** from Principe-based operators. Bom Bom Island Resort offers boat excursions but no scuba operations. Diving Principe's sites requires arrangement from Sao Tome-based operators or dedicated charters.
- **No recompression chamber in STP.** Nearest in mainland Africa or Canary Islands. Comprehensive dive insurance and evacuation coverage essential.
- No liveaboard options.
- **Portuguese** is the primary language. Atlantic Diving Center offers multi-language service; others primarily Portuguese.
- Flights via Lisbon (TAP, ~6-7 hours). Principe reached by small domestic flight from Sao Tome (~35 min, unreliable schedules).

## Season

- **Temperature:** 26–30C surface year-round. Upwelling Jun–Sep cools deeper water to 23–25C. 3mm sufficient; 5mm for upwelling-season deeper dives.
- **Two good windows with different strengths:**
  - **Jun–Sep (dry season):** calmest seas, easiest boat operations, most reliable dive access. Coincides with humpback whale season (Jul–Oct/Nov). Upwelling cools water and brings nutrients — better marine life activity. **Best overall recommendation.**
  - **Dec–Mar:** clearest underwater visibility. Warmer water. Turtle nesting peaks (Sep–Mar, but nesting activity strongest Nov–Jan). However, more rain and occasional rough seas.
- **Overlap sweet spot: Sep–Oct** — tail end of dry season calm + start of best visibility + whale season still active + turtle nesting underway. Maximizes the chance of seeing everything.
- **Avoid: Apr–May and Oct–Nov transition months** can bring unpredictable weather. Not bad, but less reliable than either main window.
- **Humpback whales:** Jul–Oct/Nov (Gulf of Guinea B1 breeding population). Boat-based watching, not in-water encounters.
- **Turtle nesting:** Sep–Mar (green, hawksbill, olive ridley, leatherback). One of the most significant nesting areas in the Gulf of Guinea.

## Conservation

- **Overfishing** is the most significant current threat — artisanal and illegal foreign industrial fleets. Shark populations already heavily depleted.
- Oil exploration concessions in the EEZ represent a future risk.
- Limited waste management, sewage into coastal waters. Plastic pollution growing.
- Enforcement capacity minimal — STP is a small, resource-poor nation.
- **Turtle conservation (MARAPA NGO)** is the most active marine conservation work. Beach patrols, nesting protection, community education.

- **This is NOT a destination for**: anyone expecting conventional reef quality or Indo-Pacific marine life density.
- **This IS a destination for**: divers who want something genuinely different, underwater photographers documenting under-recorded marine life, combined dive/cultural/nature travelers (birding, hiking, chocolate plantations). The Sao Tome dive infrastructure is functional and professional — this is not as rough as it once was.
- **Comparison**: less rewarding for pure diving than [Cape Verde](cape-verde.md), Fernando de Noronha, or Canary Islands. More exotic and less explored. Best considered as diving-as-part-of-a-broader-trip rather than a dive-specific destination.
